Wire Sorter from recycled parts

Back to projects...

Project: Upcycling Project
Course: Honors Principles of Engineering
Duration: 24 days (February 11 2021 - March 7 2021)
Objective: Reusing thrown out materials to make usable products
Type of project: Group project (3 partners)
Documentation...

In this project, I worked with my group members Henry and Josh in order to create a product line entirely made from recycled materials while still having a use. Our group went for the theme of phone accessories and management products. Henry created a selfie stick, Josh created a phone stand, and I created a cable management system, better known as a wire sorter. In my wire sorter, I used simply one plastic water bottle, one piece of cardboard, and one rubber band to create a prototype. The sorter works by using the grooves present in many water bottle designs to separate the grooves, and using the elasticity of the rubber band to pin the wires to the cardboard to keep the wires from getting tangled with each other. A few improvements I found that could have been possible would be a different fluid to be held inside the water bottle to help it keep its shape, as well as a sturdier material than cardboard to increase strength.
